River Condition Prediction

Project Showcase by Team 7-A

View Prototypes

Problem Statement

How can we accurately predict river flow rate and conditions to enhance the safety of the public and protect wildlife, while also forecasting pollution levels downstream? Current data provided by SEPA is often difficult for the general public to interpret, especially for anticipating future conditions. There is also an increasing need to understand how the upstream flow rates impact downstream pollution. Our challenge lies in making this information more accessible, actionable, and predictive with possible integrations with weather data, AI modelling, and community-contributed observations.

Proposed Solution

Our solution focuses on simplifying river condition forecasting using a combination of existing SEPA data, weather integration, and user-contributed data. By applying AI models and intuitive visualisations, we aim to provide real-time and predictive insights into river safety and pollution trends. This will empower the public, local authorities, and environmental agencies with timely and clear information.

Justification

Our solution is justified by the urgent need for readily available, reliable, and predictive information on river condition, especially under climate change and risks of pollution. SEPA information currently available, though comprehensive, is not user-friendly to the public at large and lacks predictive quality. Our application of AI modelling in the treatment of weather information enhances the predictability of river behaviour patterns in the future, which is critical to both public safety and environmental planning.

By integrating community-contributed observations, we also integrate a citizen science component that improves not just data coverage, but also public engagement and awareness. The simple and intuitive visualisations employed ensure that complex data is translated into actionable insights, rendering the platform useful to a broad range of stakeholders—from residents to environmental agencies.

This multi-faceted solution addresses the root problems of inaccessibility and unpredictability in current systems and offers a solution that is technically feasible and socially useful.

Sean Lee Wei Chuan (2957897L)

When I joined this OIP project, I discovered communication was highly important for us. We needed to stay in contact from beginning to end, know what was going on, and figure out problems fast. I learned asking the right questions assists us in comprehending our issues more clearly and knowing more about deeper beliefs. I also discovered that creating multiple prototypes and testing out various ideas with feedback assisted us. This became a common activity for implementing integrative thinking, which is keeping various prototype ideas in balance and incorporating the better parts together for better solutions. We also developed a collaborative sense of our goals, roles, and work processes at the same time. This assisted us in knowing what the other needed at various times and operating effectively together. Throughout this journey, I have learned a lot of soft skills that could enhance my software engineering role in the future which I felt deeply appreciated.

Heng Xu Yang (2957874H)

Through this project, I learned the importance of starting with a strong introduction and the appropriate questions to uncover true user needs. Building up a common mental model within our team brought us all together and helped align our vision and decision-making. Prototyping early and often allowed us to rapidly test concepts, and an opposable mind allowed us to entertain and combine opposing perspectives. Usability testing was instrumental in validating our hypotheses and iteratively refining the user experience. Together, these practices deepened our understanding of human-centered design and cemented our final solution.
